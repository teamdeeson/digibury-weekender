The festival

Organised by digital agency Deeson and hot on the heels of the brilliant first Digibury Weekender in 2014, this year's festival will be a fascinating and challenging canter through how digital is changing the world we live in. We're lining up a top notch range of speakers to fire up your synapses and entertain you in equal measure.

Hosted by Robin Ince

Comedian, writer and co-host of BBC R4’s Infinite Monkey Cage welcomes you to the Digibury Weekender as host of this one-day tech, digital, art and design festival.

Has technology made the world a better place? Have we lost the human in the rush?

We’ll be exploring how digital has made people, communities and nations more inclusive - as well as challenging this notion by looking at where new barriers are emerging through digital innovation.

What alternatives now exist that have opened up opportunities for people and for organisations?

And what is proving increasingly difficult as a result of our increased preference for using technology in the every day?

Who gets to decide what metrics matter now?

What does it mean, today, to be human?

Who's behind Digibury?

Run by digital agency, Deeson, Digibury is held at The Gulbenkian art centre, University of Kent, every second Wednesday of the month.

What is Digibury?

Put simply, Digibury is an innovative platform highlighting and celebrating the impact tech on our wider society. Free and open to the whole community we invite speakers from any background to talk about their passion and ideas.

Why Digibury?

Digibury aims to beak down the silos of technology, design, media, society, art and culture. We aim to engage and pull together the creative and technology 'clusters' that are growing and thriving in the South East... and beyond.

About Adam

Adam is Chair of the British Interactive Media Association and a Partner at Cact.us Consultants – the UK’s leading digital agency growth hacker. Prior to this he was CEO of Weapon7, and before this he was founding partner of Saint – both leading leading network agencies.

Back in ’98, he founded the world’s first full service, music on demand, internet radio station – iChooseRadio.com and ran a small youth marketing agency that specialized in the music industry. He is still passionate about music, technology and supporting the UK digital economy.

Simon Hughes

About Simon

Simon is a Trustee of stayuplate.org - “we believe that people with learning disabilities have the right to stay up late and have some fun.”

He aspired to be a professional cyclist before realising this was way beyond all his abilities... he still pretends. He trained as a psychologist before realising he was rubbish at it, but it helped him get to be involved in some interesting organisations.

Simon started work in mental heath charities then moved on to housing and other services for vulnerable people, with brief forays into Government to advise on policy.

Currently he is involved in working alongside public, private and not for profit organisations to help them connect people to make interesting things happen: Gig Buddies is a global movement, using people's shared interest to do, as he says, 'the obvious'.

About Simon

Simon Wardley is a Researcher for the Leading Edge Forum and his focus is on the intersection of IT strategy and new technologies - recently delivering a keynote at OSCON.

Simon currently leads their research entitled The Clash of the Titans. Simon's most recent published research is entitled Of Wonders and Disruption where he attempts to predict the nature of technological and business change over the next 20 years.

His previous research covers topics including The Future is More Predictable Than You Think - A Workbook for Value Chain Mapping and Beware of Geeks Bearing Gifts: Strategies for an Increasingly Open Economy.

Simon is a seasoned executive who has spent the last 15 years defining future IT strategies for companies, from Canon’s early leadership in the cloud computing space in 2005 to Ubuntu’s recent dominance as the #1 Cloud operating system.

Alina Triggs

About Alina

Alina focuses on using research to develop and guide communications strategy - helping clients to solve difficult problems, overcome reputational challenges, and better understand their stakeholders. Having started her career in politics, Alina went on to help establish the first international office of strategic polling consultancy Penn Schoen Berland, applying political methodologies to corporate campaigns.

Alina joined Freuds in November 2012. As well as working with clients to provide evidence-based recommendations, she also uses qualitative and quantitative research to provide insight and help inform the agency’s thinking.

Code of conduct

Digibury Weekender is an inclusive event. It’s based on treating all individuals respectfully, regardless of age, accessibility, ethnicity, gender or sexual orientation.

We know it’s unlikely, but if you see harassment in any form - or experience it yourself - then come and find one of the volunteer team. You’ll know who they are because they’ll be in a Digibury Weekender volunteer tee. Any harassing behaviour will result in the immediate expulsion from Digibury Weekender of the offending individual or individuals. And no refund. End of.

You can also grab one of our crack Digibury Weekender team members – Lizzie Hodgson or Liz Flynn. They’re nice and friendly. There’s also the lovely Jake Taylor from The Gulbenkian. We’re here. For you.

If you need to get hold of anyone really quickly, then text either Lizzie (07787 906 259) or Liz (07402 626 772). It doesn’t matter which – and on the positive, at least you’ll get our name right!
