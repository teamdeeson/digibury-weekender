Who's behind Digibury?

Run by digital agency, Deeson, Digibury is held at The Gulbenkian art centre, University of Kent, every second Wednesday of the month.

What is Digibury?

Put simply, Digibury is an innovative platform highlighting and celebrating the impact tech on our wider society. Free and open to the whole community we invite speakers from any background to talk about their passion and ideas.

Why Digibury?

Digibury aims to beak down the silos of technology, design, media, society, art and culture. We aim to engage and pull together the creative and technology 'clusters' that are growing and thriving in the South East... and beyond.

Howard Griffin

University of Kent — Co-Programme Director of the Architectural Visualisation Master's Degree

@howard_griffin

The talk: The Future of the Past: Regional engagement in virtual heritage

Virtual Heritage is a common term used to describe many forms of the digital representation of historical sites and buildings. The increased capability of computers and software has enabled the visualisation of this work to achieve heightened levels of realism. Howard Griffin, Programme Director of the MA Architectural Visualisation course at the University of Kent, will showcase the work his students have been creating using the latest technologies, working with regional heritage partners. Using this experience, Howard will present an argument for the increased use of technology to help develop a 'sense of place' in a virtual world. Howard will also be joined by Rowena Willard-Wright, form English Heritage.

About Howard

Howard Griffin has been involved with architecture and animation for 15 years. A graduate of the School of Architecture at London South Bank University, he quickly developed his skills in architectural presentation and animation in local authority architectural practice and later in private practice. He went on to develop his skills in animation and film graduating from the Sir John Cass School of Art with a Masters in Digital Moving Image.

Code of conduct

Digibury Weekender is an inclusive event. It’s based on treating all individuals respectfully, regardless of age, accessibility, ethnicity, gender or sexual orientation.

We know it’s unlikely, but if you see harassment in any form - or experience it yourself - then come and find one of the volunteer team. You’ll know who they are because they’ll be in a Digibury Weekender volunteer tee. Any harassing behaviour will result in the immediate expulsion from Digibury Weekender of the offending individual or individuals. And no refund. End of.

You can also grab one of our crack Digibury Weekender team members – Lizzie Hodgson or Liz Flynn. They’re nice and friendly. There’s also the lovely Jake Taylor from The Gulbenkian. We’re here. For you.

If you need to get hold of anyone really quickly, then text either Lizzie (07787 906 259) or Liz (07402 626 772). It doesn’t matter which – and on the positive, at least you’ll get our name right!

Enjoy!
